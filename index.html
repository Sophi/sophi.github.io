The reality check

Why independent validation matters

Internal QA teams are invaluable, but they're also close to the product. Too close, sometimes. They know the workarounds, they've internalized the quirks, and they might miss what a fresh user will stumble over immediately.

That's where external validation comes in. We test with fresh eyes, real-world scenarios, and zero assumptions. We catch the documentation gap, the confusing setup step, the accessory that doesn't quite fit. The embarrassing details that slip through when everyone's too familiar with the product.

Because fixing problems after launch costs more than catching them before.
